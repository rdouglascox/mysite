--- title: Welcome ---

I'm a philosopher based at the University of Sydney in the Discipline of Philosophy in the School of Humanities.

I am interested in a wide range of philosophical topics, but tend to think and write about issues in the following three double-barrelled areas: moral and political philosophy, the philosophy of mind and action, and the philosophy of logic and language.

I have recently written on the relationship between democracy and social equality and on questions related to political legitimacy and Indigenous justice. I have also written on philosophical issues relating to our knowledge of our own minds, in particular our knowledge of our own reasons for acting and holding particular attitudes, and on issues relating to the syntax and semantics of why-interrogatives. To find out more about my published research, visit my research page.

I am currently working on philosophical issues relating to educational justice, political legitimacy, the nature of political philosophy, first-person thought, and the role of perspectival thought in the humanities. To find out more about my current research visit my research page.

I regularly teach two units at the University of Sydney, PHIL2635 Contemporary Political Philosophy and the July Intensive iteration of PHIL1012 Introductory Logic. I often teach into other courses, like PHIL1013 Society, Knowledge and Self, and PHIL3686 Core Issues in Practical Philosophy. To find out more about my teaching visit my teaching page.

I live in Sydney with my partner and our three children.

You can find me on PhilPapers here and on PhilPeople here.
